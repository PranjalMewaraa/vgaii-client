# VGAII — Sales Playbook (v1)

A working document. Update as we learn. The point is to keep the next call simple, not to be exhaustive.

---

## The product in one sentence

A patient-acquisition and reputation system for ad-running clinics: every Google/Meta ad lead lands in one CRM, AI-driven automations keep each lead in the loop until they book or politely opt out, bookings flow through Cal.com, and after the visit the happy ones get nudged toward a 5-star Google review while the unhappy ones get caught privately — never reaching public Google reviews.

## The two stories that close deals

Every prospect should walk away remembering exactly two things:

### 1. "AI keeps your leads in the loop so you don't lose them."
Most clinics lose 30–50% of paid leads because nobody calls in time, the call is missed, or the follow-up never happens. Behind the scenes, our automations chase every lead — auto-acknowledge on capture, follow-up nudges on the right cadence, smart routing to the staff member who's free, re-engagement on stale leads. The receptionist gets a clean to-do list instead of a chaotic inbox. Lead slip-through drops dramatically.

### 2. "No bad review goes directly to Google."
After every visit, the patient receives a private feedback link. If they rate 4 or 5 stars, they're guided to leave a Google review. If they rate 1 or 2 stars, the review stays internal — it routes to the clinic owner privately, who can call back the same day, fix the issue, and turn an angry patient into a loyal one. Public reviews skew positive; internal feedback becomes operational gold.

These are the only two ideas that need to land in the demo. Everything else is detail.

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

We sell to:

- **Multi-doctor clinics** — 3+ practitioners, or a chain of 2+ locations.
- **Already spending ≥ ₹50,000/month on Google/Meta ads.**
- **Specialties with high LTV per patient**: dental implants & ortho, fertility/IVF, dermatology aesthetics, cosmetic surgery, hair restoration.
- **Tier-1 cities first**: Bangalore, Mumbai, Pune, Delhi NCR, Hyderabad.
- **Have a clinic manager / receptionist** (not just a doctor running everything alone).

We **do not** sell to:

- Solo dentists with one chair, no ad spend, no manager.
- Hospitals (different buyer, longer cycle, RFP-heavy).
- Practices with ≤ ₹15k/month total ad spend — they can't justify ₹10k/mo and they'll churn.
- Clinics that ask "can we do everything on WhatsApp?" — they're not ready.

If a prospect doesn't fit the ICP, the right answer is **"call us in 6 months when your ad spend crosses ₹50k."** Holding this line is the single most important sales discipline at our price point.

The only "discount" is the annual prepay. That's it.

## ROI math (the only slide that matters)

Carry this calculation in your head. Adapt to the prospect on the call.

```
Their monthly ad spend       :  ₹50,000
Leads/month at ₹500 CPL      :  100
Industry no-show / lost rate :  35%
Leads currently lost         :  35
Avg revenue per converted    :  ₹15,000  (vary by specialty)
Patients we recover / month  :  35 × 20% conversion = 7
Additional revenue / month   :  7 × ₹15,000 = ₹1,05,000
Our cost / month             :  ₹10,000
Net gain / month             :  ₹95,000  (≈ 9.5x ROI)
```

Specialty cheat-sheet for "avg revenue per patient":

| Specialty | Avg first-visit revenue | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Dental implants | ₹40k–₹1.5L | 1 patient pays for years of subscription. |
| Ortho (braces) | ₹40k–₹80k | 12-month payment plan typical. |
| Fertility / IVF | ₹1.5L–₹3L | Highest LTV, lowest volume. |
| Dermatology aesthetics | ₹3k–₹15k per visit, 3–6 visits | Rebooking is the win. |
| Cosmetic surgery | ₹50k–₹3L | High consideration, long sales cycle for clinic too. |
| Hair restoration | ₹40k–₹1.5L | Single procedure, high margin. |

If your math doesn't show ≥ 5x ROI, **re-qualify** — that prospect won't convert, and if they do they'll churn at month 4.

## Discovery call (15–30 minutes, before the demo)

Goal: confirm ICP fit, learn enough to make the demo feel custom. Don't pitch yet.

Open with: *"Before I show you anything, can I understand how patient acquisition works for you today?"*

Required answers before you proceed:

1. How many practitioners? How many locations?
2. Where do new patients come from today (Google Ads, Meta, referrals, walk-ins)? Rough split?
3. How much do you spend on ads per month?
4. Who answers the phone when a lead calls? What if they miss it?
5. How do you collect feedback today? How do you handle bad reviews?
6. What's your Google rating? How many reviews?
7. What software are you using right now (if anything)?
8. Who else is involved in this decision?

**Disqualify** if:
- Total ad spend < ₹15k/month.
- They say "the doctor handles everything personally."
- They've already evaluated 3+ competitors and want a feature checklist.
- They ask for the price before answering anything.

Disqualifying is good — saves both sides time. Send them a short note: *"Based on what you shared, we're probably not the right fit yet. Reach out when ad spend crosses ₹50k/month."*

## The demo (20 minutes max)

**Always import their data first.** Take 10 minutes before the call to put their actual patient list (CSV from their existing system, or even WhatsApp contacts) into a sandbox. Walking them through *their* clinic name, *their* patients converts 2–3× better than a generic demo.

Slide order:

1. **Reputation tab** (90 seconds). Their Google rating today. Internal feedback flow. *"Here's what happens when a 4 or 5 star comes in vs. a 1 or 2 — the bad review never reaches Google. It comes to you privately so you can call them back the same day."*
2. **Lead capture + AI automations** (3 minutes). Show a Google ad lead arriving via webhook → assigned to a staff member → automation kicks off. *"Behind the scenes, our AI keeps this lead in the loop — auto-acknowledge, follow-up at the right cadence, re-engage if it goes cold. Your team isn't doing this by hand."*
3. **Patient detail + booking** (3 minutes). Open a patient → click "Book appointment" → Cal.com loads with their phone pre-filled → the appointment lands on the calendar tab.
4. **Mark visited + collect feedback** (2 minutes). Mark visited, attach a prescription PDF, send the post-visit feedback link. *"This link is the gate — patients can only leave a Google review through it after a 4 or 5. Anything below comes to you."*
5. **Dashboard** (2 minutes). Reputation panel. Reports panel showing the funnel. Patient ratings histogram. "This is what your owner sees Monday morning."
6. **Public profile + custom domain** (90 seconds). Their clinic at `their-clinic.com` — pulls Google rating, embeds Cal.com, captures leads.
7. **The ROI slide** (2 minutes). Run the numbers using *their* ad spend.

End with: *"Should I send you the proposal? Annual or pilot?"* Always end with a closed question.

## Objection handling

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "₹10k is expensive." | "It is — for someone not running ads. You're spending `[X]` on ads every month and losing 35% of those leads. The AI automations alone pay back in `[Y]` extra patients a month. If they don't, we're a bad fit." |
| "How is this AI? Sounds like just automation." | "The AI sits in the follow-up loop — deciding when to re-engage a stale lead, what cadence works for which source, when to escalate to a real human, when to trigger the review prompt after a visit. The receptionist sees a clean to-do list. The system handles the timing and the chase." |
| "What stops a patient leaving a 1-star review on Google directly?" | "Nothing stops them — but our flow gets to them first. After every visit they get a private feedback link. If they're upset, that link captures the complaint internally before they think to vent on Google. Most upset patients use the first prompt that arrives. We make sure ours arrives first." |
| "We already use [WhatsApp / Practo / spreadsheet]." | "Great — keep using what works. We're not a replacement for clinical records, we're the layer between your ads and your calendar. The ROI is on the leads you're losing today." |
| "Send me a proposal, I'll decide later." | "Happy to. Before I send it — what's the one thing in the demo that, if it works for you, makes this an easy yes? I'll write the proposal around that." |
| "Can you do a 1-month trial?" | "We offer a 90-day pilot at ₹25k. That's 50% less than the annual. It applies as credit if you continue. Most clinics decide inside 6 weeks." |
| "Need to discuss with my partner / doctor." | "Of course. Want me to send a 1-page summary you can forward? I can also join the call with them next week — saves you the explanation." |
| "We don't run ads, we work on referrals." | "Then we're not the right fit yet. Most of our value is on paid lead recovery. If you start running ads, call me back." |
| "Can you build [custom feature]?" | "Not today, but I'll add it to the roadmap. If it's a deal-breaker, we should pause — you'll be unhappy with what we have today." |

The trap to avoid: trying to win every objection. Some are real disqualifications. Treat them as such.

## Closing tactics

- **Always ask twice.** First "no" usually means "not yet" or "I don't understand the price." Re-anchor on ROI, ask again.
- **Annual prepay is the default offer.** Quote it first. Let them downgrade themselves to monthly if they can't commit.
- **Send the proposal within 2 hours of the demo.** While the demo is fresh.
- **Follow up at +3 days, +7 days, +14 days.** Then stop. Don't drip forever.
- **Lock the price in writing.** "₹10k/month, valid until `[date]`" prevents post-call shopping.
- **Set the kickoff date in the proposal.** "Onboarding starts `[Monday + 7 days]`" creates a soft deadline.

## Channel playbook

Rough order of efficiency for the first year:

### 1. Direct outreach to ad-running clinics (highest priority)
- Search Google Maps for "implant dentist Bangalore" / "IVF clinic Mumbai" etc.
- Filter for clinics with active Google Business profiles, > 30 reviews, < 4.5 rating.
- Find the manager's WhatsApp via the clinic's contact page or Justdial.
- DM (not call): *"Are you losing 30%+ of your Google Ad leads because nobody picks up? 2-min video here."* — 60-second screen-record demo.
- Target: 50 outbound DMs / week. 5 replies, 2 demos, 1 close = realistic conversion.

### 2. Marketing agencies that serve doctors
- Find agencies that already manage Google Ads for clinics in your target specialty.
- Pitch: white-label or referral. They sell to 30 clinics; you sell once.
- Revenue split: 70/30 in your favor with 6-month exclusivity. Negotiable to 60/40 if they bring a roster of 10+.
- Expect 1–2 partnerships in 6 months. Each can be worth 5–10 customers.

### 3. Specialty associations & conferences
- IDA (Indian Dental Association) chapters, ISAR (fertility), ASI (cosmetic surgery), IADVL (derm).
- Goal: one talk per quarter. 30 minutes on "how clinics in your category are using software to convert ad spend better."
- Booth + take-home one-pager + demo signups. 5–10 trial signups per event is realistic.

### 4. Existing-customer referrals
- Activate at customer #5+. Don't bother before — you don't have density.
- Offer: ₹3,000 credit each side. Keep it simple, don't overthink the structure.
- Request: at the 3-month happy-customer milestone, ask for two warm intros by name. Doctors know doctors in the same city.

### Channels to skip in v1

- SEO / blog content — too slow, doctors don't search.
- LinkedIn ads — wrong audience, expensive.
- Google Ads for ourselves — cannibalizing the same channel our customers pay for.
- Generic cold email — opens are abysmal; WhatsApp is the channel.
- Speaking at general "SaaS" or "founder" events — irrelevant audience.

## Onboarding (the first 30 days = the product)

For the first 30 customers, treat onboarding as part of the product, not "support."

- **Day 0**: kickoff call (45 min). Webhook key issued. Custom domain wired. Cal.com connected.
- **Day 1–7**: white-glove migrate their existing patient list (CSV import). Train two staff members. Sit in for a real lead intake.
- **Day 8–30**: weekly check-in call. Compare lead capture / appointments / Google rating against the prior month.
- **Day 30**: send the delta as a single WhatsApp: *"Last month vs. this month: +`[N]` leads captured, +`[M]` Google reviews, rating moved from `[X]` to `[Y]`."* This is your renewal hook.

Around customer 20–30 you'll see patterns and can templatize / partly automate. Don't try to skip this until then.

## Metrics to watch (just three)

- **Trial → paid conversion at 30 days.** Target ≥ 50%. Below that = wedge messaging is wrong.
- **Net Google review velocity per customer.** Target +5 reviews/month after 60 days. Below that = product UX problem in the feedback flow; fix before scaling sales.
- **Logo churn at month 6.** Target < 10%. Above = customer is being abandoned, usually because the staff member who set it up left. Fix with re-training, not features.

Resist building a 20-metric dashboard before customer #30. These three answer "is this working?"

## Single biggest mistake we will make

Trying to sell to everyone. Each call to a non-ICP prospect is 30–60 minutes you didn't spend on someone who'd actually pay. Filter ruthlessly. The "no" we *don't* deliver costs more than the "no" we do.
